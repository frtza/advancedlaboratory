\section{Theory}
\label{sec:theory}

The Pythagorean theorem describes aspect ratios.

\subsection{General}

We can always write
\begin{align}
	\label{eqn:general}
	\stepcounter{equation}
	a^2 + b^2 &= c^2 \tag{1a} \\
	a^2 &= c^2 - b^2 \tag{1b}
\end{align}
for any right triangle.

\subsection{Specific}

With $a = b$ follows from the general case \eqref{eqn:general} that
\begin{equation}
	\label{eqn:specific}
	2a^2 = c^2
\end{equation}
must hold.
